Japanese Street Racing

Brief Intro

Japanese street racing consists of two popular styles: Wangan and Touge, each with unique characteristics and car culture roots.

Wangan Racing

Wangan Racing refers to racing on long expressways with high-speed stability.
It was popular in the 80s and 90s, and gained even more heat from the Mid Night Club.
It started when car enthusiasts in the 70s got their hands on supercars from that era.
Cars like Toyota 2000GT which is the "first Japanese Supercar", they decided to race
in non-policed routes like Wangan Highway or the Shuto Expressway.
In the 90s, a group emerged called the Mid Night Club, they were racers in tuners and supercars.
They announced races through cryptic texts, but after an accident in 1999, they only do track events.

Popular cars: RUF Yellowbird, Lamborghini Countach, Ferrari Testarossa, Toyota Supra, Nissan GT-R, Mazda RX-7

Touge

Touge refers to mountain pass racing focused on handling and cornering skill.
It started when minimum wage workers of Japan in the 60s wanted to "test"
their regular cars on mountain passes. Back then, most cars were budget family cars,
like station wagons and such. In the 90s, these cars evolved to more high performance Japanese vehicles,
for example, the Toyota Supra, Honda NSX.
Also in the 90s, Initial D was invented and the anime gave newfound popularity to Touge Racing.

Popular cars: AE86, RX7, Honda NSX, Toyota Supra
